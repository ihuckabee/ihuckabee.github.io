About Me

I am an undergraduate astrophysics major at Arizona State University in the School of Earth and Space Exploration currently working with Aishwarya Iyer and Dr. Michael Line.

In 2020, I began working on Project SKY-SURF with Dr. Rogier Windhorst. In the summer of 2020, I had the wonderful opportunity of working remotely with Dr. Hong Zhao at UC Boulder on radiation belt electrons. In the fall, I was awarded a NASA Space Grant scholarship to work on low-gravity M-dwarfs. I was subsequently awarded the Barry Goldwater Fellowship for this work in 2021. That year I also received the DAAD-RISE fellowship to carry out astronomy-related machine learning work at the LSW in Heidelberg, Germany. I will be working at the NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory on the Exoplanet Watch project in the summer of 2022, and will be completing my undergraduate thesis in the coming academic year.

The Big Questions...

Frankly, the vastness of space terrifies me. But, it still leaves me wondering. Like a horror movie character who decides to check out the source of evil noises by venturing into the dark basement. Below are the questions I'd like to answer by going into the dark basement.

How can we expect to characterize exoplanets accurately if we cannot even characterize their host stars correctly?

Brown dwarfs. What’s their deal? How can we use the data we have on brown dwarfs to model and characterize large exoplanets too?

If neptune-sized planets are so common, why haven’t we fully explored our own neptune-sized planets yet?

What’s the best way to process the sheer amount of astronomical data we have? How can we use machine learning processes to the fullest in astronomy?

(Note: This list is not exhaustive and always under construction)

The Even Bigger Questions...

What does a more diverse, equitable, inclusive, and accessible STEM community look like, and how do we make it a reality? How can we teach physics in a way that excites students rather than deters them? How can we open the doors of astrophysics to students in areas without considerable resources?

The Access Network connects university programs across the country dedicated to answering these questions: https://accessnetwork.org/
